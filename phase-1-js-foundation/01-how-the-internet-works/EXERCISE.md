## Your First Challenge

No coding yet — this one tests whether you **truly understood** the concept, not just memorized words.

That's actually harder than it sounds.

---

### Answer these 5 questions in your own words:

**Q1.** You open Twitter and your feed loads. Who is the **client**? Who is the **server**?

**Q2.** You fill out a signup form and click "Create Account." What HTTP **method** should that request use, and why?

**Q3.** You try to visit a profile page for a user that doesn't exist. What **status code** should the server return?

**Q4.** Look at this URL:
```
https://api.github.com/users/torvalds/repos
```
What do you think this request is asking for? Describe it in plain English.

**Q5.** True or False — and explain why:
> *"The server can send a response to the client at any time, even without a request."*

---

Take your time. Write your answers out — don't look anything up. I want to see how you understood it, not how Google explains it. After writing the solution you can cross check it with claude or gemini.
